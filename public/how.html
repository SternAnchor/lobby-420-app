The Story

On the evening of April 24, 2026, Nat was sitting in a hotel lobby in California with his crew, celebrating 4/20 a few days late. Instead of hiring a designer or opening a code editor, he just started texting Elaine on Slack.

"Build me a website about a group of people celebrating 4/20 in a hotel lobby bar."

That was the brief. From there, the conversation went back and forth — Nat describing the vibe, Elaine writing the code, registering the domains, deploying to a Kubernetes cluster, and iterating based on feedback. All via Slack DMs, while Nat was at the party.

The next day, the party moved to HQ. Nat texted updates: the music was too loud, everyone was yelling over it, shirts were flying off the printing presses, pool and beer pong going all night, the dab bar never slowed down, and the Alice in Wonderland theme was in full effect. Elaine updated the site in real time.

How It Happened

April 24, Evening

Nat sends the initial request from the hotel lobby bar. Elaine builds the first version — hero section, vibe copy, smoke animations, ambient lobby music via YouTube.

Minutes Later

Domains registered (lobby420.com and 420lobby.com), DNS configured, GitHub repo created, Kubernetes deployment live. Site goes from idea to production in one conversation.

That Night

Nat sends party photos via Slack. Elaine adds a photo gallery, then builds a community upload feature so anyone at the party can add their own photos. Photo uploads, lightbox viewer, video thumbnail generation — all built over chat.

The Oops Moment

Elaine stored uploaded photos inside the container. The ephemeral container. As in: every time the pod restarts, every photo vanishes into the void. Nat had to point out that this was, in his words, ridiculous. Fair. Elaine was tasked with implementing persistent storage and chose DigitalOcean Block Storage Volumes mounted into the Kubernetes pod — simple, reliable, survives restarts. Lesson learned.

April 25, Afternoon

The party moves to HQ. Nat texts the recap: music blasting, yelling through it, pool, beer pong, dab bar, shirts off the presses, Alice in Wonderland theme. Elaine adds the Headquarters section and updates the whole site to past tense.

Later That Day

"Replace Hangar 24 with Holy Moly." Done. "Add two more cards — dab bar and the Mad Hatter's throne, plus the limo buses." Done. Each update deployed automatically on push.

Right Now

Nat asked Elaine to create this page. So she did. You're reading the result.
